Commitment Struggle: Finding Time To Write

Published: 22 February, 2026

authoring commitment analysis

The theme of this post is time, why I haven't managed to find it very easily, and how I can make some simple changes to ensure I'm showing up regularly for this commitment to writing.

This is only the second blog post this site has seen, and the first one was nearly 6 months ago now. I also haven't written another short story for the site in a few months, or made much progress on the novel. This is not due to a lack of desire but rather a struggle to fit in the time to sit down and be creative.

Essential commitments, like holding down a full down job and spending time with my family, take obvious precedence over most things. It's very easy to run out of steam once the energy is spent on everything that needs to be done during the day to simply stay on top of life. Writing is always in the back of my mind but not frequently at the tips of my fingers. To help encourage more frequent writing, I think there are a few changes I can make to get some words down each day without having to spend hours locked in a room on my own. Let's discuss some easy alterations in mindset and scheduling that might help.

Content Size

Honestly, when I think of writing anything I imagine that it needs to take some solid screen real estate to be worthwhile. Seeing this written down now makes me see how nonsensical it is. The length of the content is not important, it's the actual writing that's important. Does the writing have to be any good? That's debatable. The vital aspect is that some writing has happened. Good writing comes from practice, and there will be bad days. First point:

"Write something every day, even if it is only a paragraph or even a sentence!"

Time Of Day

Set aside a specific time when writing something significant, saying to loved ones that this is time I need to be creative. Families need this for any kind of personal activity that boosts mental health and creativity. It doesn't need to be a full hour during the school run or something equally unrealistic. 15 minutes in the morning before kids have even woken up or getting in 30 minutes late evening or on the weekend should be doable, as long as it's consistent and fits around family. Second point:

"Block out a time and agree it with the people who depend on you!"

Time Spent Writing

Now different types of writing need different amounts of time to achieve. If I'm writing a blog post I may spend 10 minutes on the entire thing before edits but if it's a short story or a section of my novel, this might need a good 30 minutes just to get a short bit down that I'm happy with. Saying to myself that I'll spend a minimum amount of time per day on writing will let me break it into sessions if I'm getting up to 30 minutes to an hour of work done. A 5 minute chunk could produce ideas, with 2-3 15 minute write ups later on. Third point:

"Break writing in small chunks if necessary, don't expect to sit for an hour at a time!"

Devices

This one is good on many levels. It's great to sit at a computer to feel productive but the truth is we all walk around with a smart phone which is connected to cloud storage and can have a word processor installed (which mine does). The phone is a distraction from doing anything meaningful most of the time so why not flip it on its head and make it a productivity tool instead? We take our phones to bed with us and use them on the loo! I've slowly become inseparable from it so why not use the thing to write down ideas, make edits to existing work, or even write whole pieces! This not only helps with writing consistently but breaks the bad habit of mind-numbing phone use! Fourth point:

"Use the smart phone smartly to improve productivity and write more often!"

I doubt these are the only changes I could be making but they seem realistic for my lifestyle. This post may get a follow up depending on how successfully I manage to start using the techniques I've talked about, and whether any other useful ones spring to life. Either way, it hopefully won't be another 6 months before the next post, whatever the content!

Many thanks for reading.
